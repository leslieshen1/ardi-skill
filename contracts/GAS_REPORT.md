## Daily operating cost projection (mainnet)

Assumptions:
- 480 epochs/day (3-min cadence)
- 100 active agents averaging 5 commits/day
- 50 fusions/day
- 200 settlement claims/day

| Actor | Daily gas | Daily $ @ 0.01 gwei |
|---|---:|---:|
| Coordinator (open + publish + request + settle) | ~1B | $10 |
| All agents (5 commits × 100 = 500 commits + 500 reveals + ~50 inscribes) | ~140M | $1.40 |
| All fusions (50 × 140k) | ~7M | $0.07 |
| All settlement claims (200 × 68k) | ~14M | $0.14 |
| **Network total** | **~1.16B** | **~$11.60/day** |

Sustainable: even at 10x traffic, daily cost remains < $120. Base gas economics
are very favorable for this design.

## Gas-related findings

1. **publishAnswer is the largest Coordinator cost** (~80k × 15 riddles = 1.2M gas/epoch).
   Batching all 15 publishes in one tx (`publishAnswerBatch(...)`) would save
   gas via warm storage slots. Defer to post-mainnet if cost becomes a concern.

2. **inscribe worst-case 218k gas** is dominated by:
   - `_safeMint` (ERC721 storage write + receiver hook ~30k)
   - Bond escrow `onMinted` hook (~30k)
   - Vault answer pull from epochDraw (~10k)
   - Inscription struct write (~80k)

   Acceptable; not worth optimizing.

3. **commit at 50k** is cheap. The bond ETH transfer dominates.

4. **VRF callback `onRandomness` 24k** is well under the 100k callback gas
   we configured for the Chainlink subscription. Plenty of headroom.
